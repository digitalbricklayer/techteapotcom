Many years ago in the late 1990s I came across a personal finance management application called Intuit in my local PC World. It promised getting hold of your finances so that you'd know where all of your money was going. I quickly picked up the box and nearly ran to the payment desk.

Who doesn't want to know where all of their money seems to disappear every month.

This started a two year period of me obsessively keeping all of my receipts and recording every financial transaction in my copy of Intuit. It didn't matter how small the transaction was, in would go the receipt into my wallet. Every time I sat down at my computer, I drag out my now plump wallet and record every last one into Intuit. I felt like I was achieving something. It felt like I was at last in control of my finances. At a glance I could find out precisely where my money was going.

Then one day, I thought: I never look at my spending.
